South Korea Events

Korean events are full of life and warmth, creating an atmosphere that’s both exciting and welcoming. There’s a special energy in the air, with people coming together to enjoy music, dance, and performances that make you feel like you're part of a big, joyful celebration. Whether it’s the beauty of wearing traditional hanbok during holidays or the fun of modern festivities lighting up the streets, there’s always a wonderful mix of tradition and new vibes. Food is at the heart of these gatherings, with shared meals and treats like those exchanged on Pepero Day, showing the kindness and generosity of Korean culture. The sights and sounds are magical, with colorful decorations, sparkling lights, and the mouth-watering aroma of street food filling the air. At the same time, many of these events have deeper meanings whether honoring ancestors or hoping for good fortune. And of course, there's a playful side to it all, like the muddy fun of the Boryeong Mud Festival, where people laugh, get messy, and just enjoy the moment. Whether in a bustling city or a peaceful village, Korean events have this wonderful way of bringing people together, celebrating culture, and creating memories that stay with you long after.

About the Events

The Annual Cultural Festival celebrates our rich heritage with traditional dances, music, and food. Join us for an unforgettable experience featuring live performances, workshops, and art exhibitions. Here are the Events and Festivals celebrated in South Korea.

Activities & Highlights

Daeboreum

Date: 15th day of the Lunar New Year (February or March).

Description: A celebration of the year's first full moon with rituals to bring health and good fortune, like cracking nuts and burning daljip (straw piles) to ward off evil spirits.

Dano

Date: 5th day of the 5th lunar month (May or June).

Description: A traditional festival marking the end of planting season, featuring traditional dances, games like ssireum (Korean wrestling), and rituals for good health.

Jinju Namgang Yudeung

Date: Early October.

Description: A stunning festival held in Jinju, featuring thousands of floating lanterns on the Namgang River to honor fallen soldiers and loved ones.

Cherry Blossom

Date: Late March to early April (varies by region).

Description: Famous cherry blossom festivals occur across Korea, including in Jinhae, Yeouido, and Gyeongju, celebrating the beauty of spring with cultural performances and picnics under blooming trees.

Boryeong Mud Festival

Date: July (specific dates vary).

Description: Held at Daecheon Beach, this fun-filled festival includes mud wrestling, slides, and spa treatments using the mineral-rich local mud.

Chuseok

Date: 15th day of the 8th lunar month (September or October).

Description: Often called "Korean Thanksgiving," families gather to honor ancestors, share harvest foods like songpyeon (rice cakes), and visit family graves.

Jinju Namgang Yudeung

Date: 15th day of the Lunar New Year (February or March).

Description: A celebration of the year's first full moon with rituals to bring health and good fortune, like cracking nuts and burning daljip (straw piles) to ward off evil spirits.

Busan International Film Festival

Date: October (dates vary).

Description: One of Asia’s most prestigious film festivals, showcasing international and Korean films with various cultural events.

Andong Mask Dance

Date: Late September to early October.

Description: Celebrates traditional Korean mask dances, featuring performances, mask-making workshops, and folk games.

Seongsan Sunrise Festival

Date: January 1st.

Description: Welcoming the New Year at Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak with a magnificent sunrise, accompanied by traditional performances and prayers for prosperity.

Jeongwol Daeboreum Fire Festival

Date: Early February (dates vary).

Description: A Jeju Island tradition involving lighting massive bonfires to celebrate the first full moon of the lunar year and ensure a bountiful harvest.

Pepero Day

Date: November 11th

Pepero Day, celebrated on November 11 in South Korea, is a lighthearted occasion where people exchange Pepero sticks as tokens of affection and good wishes, inspired by their resemblance to the date’s numeric shape (11/11).
